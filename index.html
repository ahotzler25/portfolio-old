Pomodoro Timer

GitHub Repo


DESCRIPTION: The project consists of three cipher, each unique to the other:

The Caesar Shift is a type of substitution cipher originally used by Julius Caesar to protect messages of military significance. It relies on taking the alphabet and "shifting" letters to the right or left, based on the typical alphabetic order.

The Polybius Square is a cipher that is achieved by arranging a typical alphabet into a grid. Each letter is represented through a coordinate. For example, in the above table, the letter "B" would be represented by the numerical pair "21".

The Substitution Cipher requires a standard alphabet and a substitution alphabet. Letters from the standard alphabet will be transposed to the standard alphabet. This cipher requires that the recipient have the substitution alphabet, otherwise it will be difficult for them to decode the message.

Local Library

GitHub Repo


DESCRIPTION: This project is designed to test my ability to build complex algorithms. It also focused on my ability to write unit tests with Mocha and Chai to automate tests. For all three ciphers I had to ensure to ignore letter casings and maintained spaces.

For the Caesar Shift, I had to ensure false was returned had a shift of 0, 26 or greater, or a -26 or greater was submitted. I also had to wrap around the alphabet in the event a letter shifted past "z" or behind "a".

For the Polybius Square, I had to combine the values of "i" and "j" to maintain a perfect 5x5 square of alphabetical values. "thinkful" and "thjnkful" would return the same value.

For the Substitution cipher, I had to ensure a full 26 unique characters were input for the given alphabet.



About

I'm Andy Hotzler, a full-stack software engineer and game developer located in northern Minnesota with my domestic shorthair, Dahlia. I enjoy learning, whether it be new languages, frameworks, testing methodologies, or what have you. Two heads are always better than one, and I enjoy reviewing code with colleagues to not only see how it can be improved, but also how different approaches can reach a similar solution. Working alone has advantages, but collaborating with a dedicated team cannot be matched.

After graduating from Bemidji State University with three Bachelors and a minor, I began relearning web development and followed my interests into game and software development. After almost two years of Udemy courses and tutorial hell on YouTube and freecodecamp.org, I felt like I was only placing pieces of a much larger puzzle together while being unable to see the larger picture. After researching bootcamps, curriculums, and job markets, I made the commitment enrolled in Thinkful's engineering immersion program and graduated in May 2021.

When I'm not coding or working in Unreal Engine 4 and Unity, I can be found butchering the German language, running it down Summoner's Rift in League of Legends, saving Tamriel in Elder Scrolls Online, getting decimated in Destiny 2's Crucible, or incorrectly sequencing my plays in Magic: The Gathering and Legends of Runeterra.
